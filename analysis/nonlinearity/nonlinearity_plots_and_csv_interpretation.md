# Nonlinearity Analysis: Plot Interpretation and CSV Summary

This note combines the interpretation of the figures in [`analysis/nonlinearity`](./) with a summary of the underlying CSV outputs. The objective of this section is to explain whether the relationship between indexing ownership (`ind_own`) and market quality is nonlinear, and, if so, whether there is an economically meaningful ownership range that appears beneficial.

## 1. Reduced-form nonlinear plots

[`nonlinear_relationship_all3.svg`](./nonlinear_relationship_all3.svg) is the baseline non-IV quadratic fixed-effects figure. It plots the fitted quadratic change in each outcome across the observed support of `ind_own`, relative to the median ownership level. The central result is that the relationship between indexing and market outcomes is not linear. For `volatility`, the fitted curve is U-shaped: volatility declines as indexing rises from low levels, but after a turning point the benefit weakens and eventually reverses. For `price_info`, the curve is also U-shaped, implying that measured price informativeness initially falls with indexing and only recovers at relatively high ownership levels. For `amihud_illiq`, the quadratic pattern is weaker and less economically clean because the estimated turning point lies outside the support of the data.

[`indexing_good_range_score.svg`](./indexing_good_range_score.svg) aggregates these reduced-form outcome curves into a standardized market-quality score. The score is constructed so that lower illiquidity and lower volatility increase market quality, while higher price informativeness also increases market quality. This figure therefore answers the practical question, “How much indexing is good?” The estimated composite optimum is approximately `ind_own = 0.0379`, and the top 5 percent score range is roughly `[0.0328, 0.0430]`. The economic interpretation is that low-to-moderate indexing appears to be associated with the strongest overall market quality in the reduced-form specification.

## 2. Reduced-form CSV summary

The statistical basis for the reduced-form plots is reported in [`nonlinear_quadratic_results.csv`](./nonlinear_quadratic_results.csv). For `amihud_illiq`, the linear term is small and insignificant (`b1 = 0.0107`, `p = 0.748`), while the quadratic term is positive and marginally significant (`b2 = 0.1882`, `p = 0.0469`). However, the turning point is negative and outside the observed support, so this should not be interpreted as a meaningful interior optimum for liquidity. For `volatility`, both the linear term (`b1 = -0.3250`, `p < 0.001`) and the quadratic term (`b2 = 0.9991`, `p < 0.001`) are strongly significant, yielding a turning point around `0.1626` that lies inside the support. For `price_info`, the same qualitative pattern holds: `b1 = -10.3568` and `b2 = 26.7002`, both highly significant, with a turning point around `0.1939`, also in support. These estimates confirm that nonlinearity is a first-order reduced-form feature, especially for volatility and price informativeness.

[`nonlinear_indexing_score_grid.csv`](./nonlinear_indexing_score_grid.csv) converts those estimated curves into a composite score over an `ind_own` grid. As described in [`market_score_method.md`](./market_score_method.md), the score is defined as `-z(amihud) - z(volatility) + z(price_info)`, after standardizing each outcome-specific predicted curve. The table shows that the score reaches its maximum near `ind_own = 0.0379`. The rows with `is_top5pct_score = 1` define the economically “good” ownership range, which spans approximately `0.0328` to `0.0430`. Accordingly, the score plot should be read not as a separate estimation exercise, but as a summary of the reduced-form nonlinear estimates.

## 3. IV first-stage plots

[`iv_first_stage_cutoff2000_global.svg`](./iv_first_stage_cutoff2000_global.svg) and [`iv_first_stage_cutoff1000_global.svg`](./iv_first_stage_cutoff1000_global.svg) visualize the residualized first-stage relationship between the cutoff instrument and `ind_own`. The difference between these two figures is economically important. The cutoff-2000 first stage displays a clear slope and is supported by strong first-stage diagnostics in the corresponding table, indicating that the instrument has substantial explanatory power for both `ind_own` and `ind_own^2`. By contrast, the cutoff-1000 first stage is not reliable as a nonlinear IV design because the corresponding diagnostics become unstable in the output tables. As a result, the Russell 2000 threshold should be treated as the preferred nonlinear IV design, whereas the Russell 1000 threshold functions only as a weak robustness check.

## 4. Preferred nonlinear IV plots

[`iv_quadratic_fitted_lines_cutoff2000_global.svg`](./IV/iv_quadratic_fitted_lines_cutoff2000_global.svg) is the cleanest nonlinear IV figure. It plots the fitted quadratic IV component for each outcome over the observed support of `ind_own`. The most credible result is the `volatility` panel, which displays a pronounced U-shape: volatility falls as indexing rises from low levels, reaches a minimum at an interior turning point, and then rises again as indexing becomes larger. The `amihud_illiq` panel also shows curvature, but the shape is less straightforward because only the quadratic term is statistically significant. The `price_info` panel appears comparatively flat and unstable, which is consistent with the lack of statistical significance in the associated IV table.

[`iv_fitted_indownhat_vs_depvars_cutoff2000_global.svg`](./iv_fitted_indownhat_vs_depvars_cutoff2000_global.svg) presents the same IV idea more visually by plotting residualized outcomes against first-stage fitted ownership. This figure is best understood as a descriptive complement to the coefficient table rather than the primary inferential object. The volatility panel again provides the clearest visual evidence of nonlinearity. The liquidity panel suggests some curvature, but the signal is weaker, and the price-informativeness panel remains too diffuse to justify a strong conclusion. The analogous cutoff-1000 figure, [`iv_fitted_indownhat_vs_depvars_cutoff1000_global.svg`](./iv_fitted_indownhat_vs_depvars_cutoff1000_global.svg), should not be emphasized because the underlying IV specification is weak and unstable.

## 5. IV CSV summary

The preferred nonlinear IV estimates are reported in [`iv_quadratic_cutoff2000_global.csv`](./iv_quadratic_cutoff2000_global.csv). This table is important because it shows that the first stage is strong for both endogenous terms. The joint first-stage F-statistics for `ind_own` are approximately `639`, `611`, and `664` across the three dependent variables, while the corresponding F-statistics for `ind_own^2` are approximately `28`, `26`, and `34`. This indicates that the instrument is sufficiently strong in the global Russell 2000 design to support nonlinear inference.

In the second stage, the strongest result concerns `volatility`. The coefficient on `ind_own` is negative and highly significant (`b1 = -2.5336`, `p = 0.00019`), while the coefficient on `ind_own^2` is positive and highly significant (`b2 = 15.6492`, `p = 0.00038`). The estimated turning point is `0.0809`, which lies inside the observed support. Economically, this implies that indexing reduces volatility at low ownership levels, but the benefit eventually weakens and reverses as ownership rises further. For `amihud_illiq`, the linear term is insignificant (`b1 = 0.3313`, `p = 0.385`), but the quadratic term is negative and significant (`b2 = -4.8236`, `p = 0.0366`), with a turning point around `0.0343`. This suggests some nonlinear liquidity effect, but the economic shape is less stable than for volatility. For `price_info`, neither the linear nor the quadratic term is significant, so the preferred IV design does not provide robust evidence of a nonlinear effect on price informativeness.

[`iv_quadratic_cutoff1000_global.csv`](./iv_quadratic_cutoff1000_global.csv) serves mainly as a cautionary comparison. Some of the second-stage coefficients appear large, but the associated first-stage diagnostics are numerically unstable, including negative joint F-statistics in the comparison outputs. That makes the cutoff-1000 nonlinear IV specification economically unreliable. The direct comparison in [`iv_quadratic_cutoff1000_vs_2000_comparison.csv`](./iv_quadratic_cutoff1000_vs_2000_comparison.csv) therefore supports using the global Russell 2000 cutoff as the baseline nonlinear IV design.

## 6. IV robustness and local-design tables

The remaining IV tables reinforce the same conclusion. [`IV/iv_quadratic_results.csv`](./IV/iv_quadratic_results.csv), which reports a local design with `cutoff = 1000` and `bandwidth = 150`, produces first-stage F-statistics only around `1.1` to `1.5`, far below conventional relevance thresholds. None of the nonlinear coefficients in that table are statistically informative. Similarly, [`IV/iv_quadratic_grid_best_specs.csv`](./IV/iv_quadratic_grid_best_specs.csv) and [`IV/iv_quadratic_grid_results.csv`](./IV/iv_quadratic_grid_results.csv) show that even the best-performing local bandwidth and cutoff combinations achieve minimum first-stage F-statistics only around `3`. Therefore, nonlinear IV identification is generally weak in local specifications and becomes convincing only in the global Russell 2000 design.

## 7. Overall interpretation

Taken together, the plots and CSV tables deliver a coherent conclusion. In reduced-form panel fixed-effects estimates, indexing has a clearly nonlinear relationship with market quality, and the combined score suggests that the most favorable range is around `3.3` percent to `4.3` percent ownership, with an optimum near `3.8` percent. Once endogeneity is addressed using the preferred nonlinear IV design, the strongest and most credible result is a U-shaped effect on volatility under the global Russell 2000 cutoff. This implies that indexing is beneficial for volatility at low ownership levels, but that the benefit diminishes and eventually reverses as ownership becomes larger. By contrast, the liquidity effect is weaker and less stable, and the price-informativeness effect does not survive convincingly in the preferred IV specification.
